⚖️ Legal Compliance for Recruiters

Know the rules. Protect yourself, protect the candidate, protect Rylem.

✅ What You CAN Ask
👍 Questions Related to Ability to Do the Job
  • Work history, skills, certifications
  • "Are you authorized to work in the United States?"
  • "Can you perform the essential functions of this job with or without accommodation?"
  • Availability, start date, schedule flexibility
  • Salary/rate expectations
  • Willingness to travel or relocate (if job requires it)
🚫 What You CANNOT Ask
⛔ Protected Characteristics (EEOC)
  • Age / date of birth (exception: "Are you 18 or older?")
  • Race, ethnicity, national origin
  • Religion
  • Marital status, family plans, pregnancy
  • Sexual orientation, gender identity
  • Disability or health conditions
  • Arrest record (convictions may be asked in some states — check state law)
  • Military discharge status
  • Genetic information
🚨
The Rule

If it's not about their ability to do THIS job, don't ask it. When in doubt, don't ask. It's that simple.

📋 Documentation Requirements
📝 Required Paperwork
  • I-9 — within 3 business days of start
  • W-4 — before first paycheck
  • Background check consent — client-dependent
  • Drug screening consent — client-dependent
  • NDA/Non-compete — client-dependent, check with BDM
💻
Document Everything

Keep ALL candidate communications professional and documented in CEIPAL. If it's not logged, it didn't happen.

🔴 Red Lines — Immediate Escalation
🚨
Stop and Escalate Immediately

These situations require immediate action. Do not try to handle them yourself.

  • Candidate reports harassment or discrimination at a client site → Escalate to Liza immediately
  • Client asks you to filter candidates by protected characteristic → Refuse and escalate to Mike
  • Candidate threatens legal action → Stop all communication, escalate to Mike
  • You accidentally asked an illegal question → Document it, tell your manager, don't try to cover it up
