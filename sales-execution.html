📞 Cold Call Scripts
🎯
Ground Rules

A script is a framework, not a teleprompter. Internalize the structure so it sounds like a conversation, not a reading. Practice until you forget you're using a script.

The only objective of a cold call is one thing: book a 20-minute meeting. Not to explain Rylem. Not to ask about open roles. Not to pitch differentiators. Book the meeting.

📞Script 1 — Cold Call (No Prior Relationship)▼

The Opener

"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Rylem Staffing. Did I catch you at an okay time?"

Wait for response. If yes, go directly into the hook. Don't thank them for taking the call — just move.

The Hook (15 seconds)

"I'll be quick. We help engineering managers at companies like [relevant company in their space] hire contractors fast — usually within two weeks — when a need comes up. I'm not calling because I know you have an open role right now. I'm calling because the managers we work best with are ones who've already had a 20-minute conversation with us before they're in a rush."

The Ask

"Would it be worth 20 minutes this week or next to talk through what that looks like for your team?"
🤫

Stop talking. Wait. The silence is intentional — let them respond.

If They Say Yes

"Perfect. I have Thursday at 2pm or Friday morning — which works better for you?"

Always give two specific options. Open-ended scheduling kills momentum.

If They Say "What Is Rylem?" or "Tell Me More"

"Sure — we're a staffing firm that specializes in placing contractors on engineering and tech teams. We're a diverse supplier, we work nationwide, and our average time-to-fill for contract roles is under two weeks. The 20-minute call is really just so I can understand your team's setup and make sure we're useful to you when a need comes up — not a sales pitch."

Voicemail (max 20 seconds)

"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Rylem Staffing. I'll send you a quick email so you have my info — but the short version is we help engineering teams hire contractors fast and I'd love 20 minutes to introduce ourselves before you ever need us. I'll follow up by email. Talk soon."
📧

Always follow the voicemail with an email within 15 minutes referencing the call.

🤝Script 2 — Warm Call (Approved Vendor / Prior Relationship)▼

Rylem is an approved vendor or has placed with this company before. Manager may not know you personally.

The Opener

"Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Rylem Staffing. We're actually an approved vendor for your team — did I catch you at an okay time?"

The "approved vendor" line does a lot of work here. It bypasses the immediate skepticism of a cold call. Don't bury it.

The Hook

"I realized we've probably never actually talked directly, and I wanted to fix that. We've [placed contractors with your team / been working with leaders at your company] for a while now — but I'd rather have a 20-minute intro call with you before you have an open role than try to be useful when you're already in a crunch."

The Ask

"Would you have 20 minutes this week or next to connect? No agenda beyond getting to know your team's setup so we can actually be useful when a need comes up."
🤫

Same as Script 1 — stop and wait.

If They're Skeptical ("I didn't know we worked with you")

"Totally fair — it usually runs through [procurement / HR / another manager]. We've worked with [name drop one relevant leader if appropriate] on the [engineering / data / etc.] side. I'm just trying to make sure your team specifically has a direct contact at Rylem so you're not going through a process when you need someone fast."

If They Say "What do you place?"

"Primarily contractors on engineering and tech teams — software engineers, DevOps, data engineers, that kind of profile. We also do full-time when teams need it. The 20 minutes is really just so I understand what your team actually looks like and what kinds of roles come up — so I'm useful to you specifically, not just generically."

Warm Call Bonus Objection

🚧 "We go through [other contact at company] for staffing."
✅ Response
"Good to know — I'll make sure I'm coordinated with them. I still think it's worth 20 minutes directly with you, because the managers who get the best results from us are the ones who've told us what good looks like for their specific team. [Other contact] can handle the paperwork — I want to make sure I understand your needs. Does [day/time] work?"

All other objections from Script 1 apply here too.

🧠Meta-Notes — How to Deliver▼
🎙️
On Tone

Tech hiring managers respond to directness and brevity. Overly warm or salesy openers signal "vendor call" immediately. Flat, confident, and fast signals peer-level conversation.

⏱️
On Pacing

Slow down on the hook and the ask. Rushing through the value prop and the meeting request is the most common mistake. Say the ask, then be quiet.

🔄
On Rejection

A "no" on a cold call is almost never permanent. "Not right now" is the real answer 80% of the time. Log it, set a 60-day follow-up, and move on without burning the relationship.

📊
On Volume

These scripts only work if you're making enough calls. At 70 daily activities, you should expect roughly 8–12 decision-maker conversations per day and 2–3 meetings booked. If your ratios are worse than that, the script isn't the problem — the list or the delivery is.

🛡️Cold Call Objection Handling▼
🚧 "I'm not hiring right now."
✅ Response
"That's actually why I'm calling now instead of later. The managers who fill roles fastest are the ones who had this conversation before they had an open req. All I'm asking for is 20 minutes so we're not starting from scratch when the timing does change. Does [day] work?"
🚧 "We use a VMS / we go through procurement."
✅ Response
"Totally understand — a lot of companies do. Out of curiosity, does every contractor hire go through that process, or do hiring managers have any flexibility on urgent or specialized needs?"

Listen. If everything goes through VMS, qualify them out cleanly:

"Got it — appreciate you being straight with me. I'll follow up through the right channel and if direct ever makes sense, I hope you'll keep us in mind."
🚧 "We have vendors we already use."
✅ Response
"Makes sense — most teams do. I'm not trying to replace anyone. A lot of the managers we work with use us as a backup for roles their primary vendors struggle to fill, or when speed is the priority. Would it be worth 20 minutes to see if there's a fit for that kind of situation?"
🚧 "Send me an email."
✅ Response
"Happy to do that. What's the best address — and is there anything specific you'd want me to include so it's actually useful when you open it?"

Get the email address verbally. Then send within the hour while you're still in their memory.

🚧 "I'm too busy right now."
✅ Response
"I hear you — I'll make it easy. I have a 20-minute slot [specific day] at [specific time]. I'll send a calendar hold right now and if it doesn't work you can just decline. Does that work?"

"We use an MSP/VMS"

"We're not hiring right now"

"Send me an email"
✅ Response
"Happy to. What specifically would be most useful — our capabilities deck, case studies, or just a quick intro?"
🚧 Objection
"We already have a staffing vendor"
✅ Response
"Most of our clients work with 3–5 vendors. We're not trying to replace anyone — we're the diversity-certified option that fills the gaps."
🚧 Objection
"What makes you different?"
✅ Response
"Two things: speed and retention. We fill VMS roles in 12 days average, and we've had near-zero contractor attrition in 8 years. Plus we're diversity-certified, which helps your supplier diversity numbers."
📊 Lead Scoring
🎯 Prioritize Your Pipeline
🟢 HIGH Priority
Multiple open roles + recently funded + mid-size + growth mode
🟡 MEDIUM Priority
1–2 open roles + stable company + relevant industry
🔴 LOW Priority
No open roles + large enterprise (hard to break in) + no buying signals
📡 Buying Signals (Intent)
🔍 What to Watch For
  • Job postings — they're spending on talent right now
  • Competitor staffing firms posting roles for them — they're already buying contingent labor
  • Funding rounds, office expansions, new product launches — growth = headcount
  • Contractor end dates — upcoming backfill opportunities; get ahead of them
  • Layoffs — FTE-to-contractor conversion window opens 60–90 days post-layoff
