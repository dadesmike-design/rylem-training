🛂 Visa & Work Authorization Guide

Know your visa types. It prevents wasted screens, protects clients, and helps you identify candidates others overlook.

📋 Reference this on every initial screen

💡 Why Recruiters Need This

🌐 Rylem Places Talent Nationwide
💡
Two Ways Visa Knowledge Pays Off

Understanding visa types prevents wasted time screening candidates who can't legally work — AND helps you identify candidates others overlook. Both matter.

🚨
Rylem Policy — Non-Negotiable

We do NOT sponsor H1B visas. We DO work with H1B candidates on a C2C basis. Always verify work authorization during the initial screen. When in doubt, ask: "Are you legally authorized to work in the US for any employer?"

📋 Visa Types — Know All of These

🟢 Green Card / Permanent Resident
  • Can work for ANY employer — no restrictions whatsoever
  • Treat the same as US citizens for employment purposes
  • No sponsor needed, no expiration to track
✅

Green light — place them anywhere, any role, any client.

🔵 H1B Visa — The Most Common Work Visa
  • Most common work visa for skilled professionals — you'll see this constantly
  • TRANSFERABLE — candidate can change employers (unlike L1)
  • Tied to a specific employer until transferred — verify who the sponsor is
  • If they have an I-140 approved: can extend H1B indefinitely while waiting for green card
  • C2C employment possible if they have an approved I-140
❓
Key Question

"Who is your current H1B sponsor?" — You need to know this before placing them anywhere.

🔷 H4 Visa — H1B Dependent (Spouse/Children)
  • Spouse or children of an H1B holder
  • CAN work if they have an EAD (Employment Authorization Document)
  • No restriction on which employer — if the EAD is valid, they're good to go
❓
Key Question

"Do you have a valid EAD?" — Always check the EAD expiration date. A lapsed EAD = no work authorization.

🎓 F1 Visa — Student
  • Student visa — can work through OPT or CPT programs only
  • OPT (Optional Practical Training): 12 months of work authorization after degree — STEM fields get 36 months total with STEM extension
  • CPT (Curricular Practical Training): Work authorization while still enrolled in school — must be tied to coursework
  • If OPT expires and they don't get an H1B, they can't work — track those dates
❓
Key Questions

"Are you on OPT or CPT?" / "When does your EAD expire?" / "Are you applying for H1B in the next lottery?"

🍁 TN Visa — NAFTA (Canada & Mexico Only)
  • Only for Canadian and Mexican citizens — no other nationalities
  • Must change visa status every time they change employers — not transferable like H1B
  • Includes: accountants, engineers, scientists, teachers, and other specific NAFTA professions
  • Relatively straightforward process compared to H1B — no annual cap
🇨🇦

If your candidate is Canadian or Mexican, TN may be a pathway even if they're currently on another visa or between statuses.

🔴 L1 Visa — Internal Company Transfer
🚫
Do Not Recruit L1 Holders (Unless Transitioning)

L1 is NON-TRANSFERABLE. They can ONLY work for their sponsoring employer. No C2C. Do not recruit them unless they are actively getting a green card or switching to H1B.

  • Internal company transfer visa — company moves employee from overseas office to US
  • Cannot do C2C
  • Cannot change employers without changing visa status
🔷 L2 Visa — L1 Dependent (Spouse)
  • Spouse of an L1 holder
  • CAN work with a valid EAD — no employer restriction
  • These candidates are often overlooked — if they have an EAD, they're fully placeable
🦘 E3 Visa — Australia Only
  • Only for Australian citizens
  • Similar to H1B but simpler — no annual lottery cap
  • 2-year renewals, no limit on the number of extensions
  • Must change status when changing employers (similar to TN)
🪪 EAD — Employment Authorization Document
  • Physical card that proves work authorization
  • Given to H4 holders, L2 holders, F1 OPT/CPT holders, asylum seekers, refugees, and others
  • Always check the expiration date — an expired EAD = no authorization
  • Asylees and refugees have category codes C33 or C09 on their EAD
⚠️
Track the Expiration

EADs expire. If a candidate's EAD expires mid-contract, they can no longer work. Log the expiration date in CEIPAL and set a reminder to follow up before it expires.

⚡ Quick Decision Matrix

📊 Fast Reference — Use This During Screens
✅ Can Work Anywhere?
Green Card US Citizen H4 w/ EAD L2 w/ EAD Asylee/Refugee
🔄 Transferable?
H1B — YES (can change employers)
L1 — NO (locked to sponsor)
🎓 Students?
Check OPT vs. CPT status. Check EAD expiration date. Ask about H1B lottery plans.
🍁 Canada/Mexico?
TN visa is an option. Faster than H1B. Check if their profession qualifies under NAFTA.
❓
When In Doubt — Use This Exact Question

"Are you legally authorized to work in the United States for any employer without sponsorship?" — If they say yes, confirm it. If they hesitate, dig deeper.
