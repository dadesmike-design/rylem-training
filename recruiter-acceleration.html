🎯 Interview Prep Call (24-48 hrs Before Interview)
📋1. Confirm Logistics▼
  • Date, time, time zone
  • Platform (Zoom/Teams) + link or dial-in
  • Interview length and format (panel, 1:1, technical)
🎓2. Role & Company Coaching▼
  • Review the job description thoroughly with the candidate
  • Align their experience to what the role needs — have them practice specifics
  • Coach on STAR method examples (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
  • Cover company background, values, recent news
  • Brief on interviewer names/titles (pull LinkedIn profiles if possible)
  • Prepare the candidate with questions to ask the interviewer
💻3. Technical Expectations▼
  • Tools/platforms needed (HackerRank, HubSpot, Google Docs, etc.)
  • Format: live coding, take-home, portfolio review
  • Time limits and structure
👔4. Logistics & Professionalism▼
  • Be early — test link 5-10 minutes before
  • Test internet, camera, mic, software
  • Find a quiet, professional setting — professional background
  • Dress code (especially for onsite)
📱5. Follow-Up▼
  • Send motivational message morning-of (or night before if early interview)
  • Offer to be available 15 minutes before the interview
  • Ask candidate to text confirming they're set and ready

📋 Post-Interview Debrief (Within 24 Hours)
🚨
This MUST be a phone call — not text, not email.

Rylem requirement. Schedule the debrief during the prep call so the candidate knows it's coming.

🎯1. Interest & Fit▼
  • "After learning more about the role, how did you feel the interview went?"
  • "Has your interest level changed since speaking with the hiring team directly?"
  • "On a scale of 1 to 'I really want this job,' where would you put yourself?"
🔍2. Process & Clarity▼
  • "Was anything unclear or confusing during the interview?"
  • "Did you get to ask the questions you wanted to?"
  • "Is there anything you feel could've gone better — or differently?"
  • "Was there anything we could've done to help you prepare more effectively?"
🕵️3. Intel Gathering▼
  • "Did the interviewer mention next steps or give any hints?"
  • "Any specific feedback or discussion points that stood out?"
  • "What questions were you asked? Anything that caught you off guard?"
  • "Any behavioral, situational, or technical questions worth noting?"
⚔️4. Competitive Activity▼
  • "Have there been any changes in your interview activity with other companies?"
  • "Any other roles you're progressing with in terms of timing or offers?"

  • Know the status of EVERY candidate you're working with
  • Every candidate needs a next step and a date — no "I'll follow up sometime"
  • Update CEIPAL daily — your pipeline should be accurate at all times
  • Weekly pipeline review: what moved, what stalled, what's new
  • Communicate proactively to BDMs — don't wait for them to ask
⭐ Candidate Experience — This Is Your Brand
  • Respond to every candidate within 24 hours
  • If you don't have an update, tell them: "No news yet, but I'm following up with the client today"
  • Never ghost a candidate — even if they didn't get the role
  • Rejection calls > rejection emails. Be direct, be kind, offer to keep them in mind
  • After placement: check in after first day, then weekly for first month
📋 Working Multiple Reqs
  • Prioritize by: time-to-fill urgency, client importance, margin, fill difficulty
  • Don't spread yourself thin — better to fill 3 reqs well than work 10 poorly
  • Reuse candidates across reqs — someone rejected for one role might be perfect for another
  • Flag candidates to BDMs proactively: "I have someone strong who didn't work for [Client A] but would be great for [Client B]"

🚩 Red Flags — Auto-Escalate
  • Talking more than 60% of the call (recruiter should listen more than talk)
  • Accepted "yes" without follow-up validation
  • Revealed top of pay range unprompted
  • Submitted a candidate 5+ years outside the JD experience range
  • Debrief done via text/email instead of call
  • Debrief not done within 24 hours
  • Didn't ask about competing offers
  • Skipped counter offer discussion (candidate currently employed)
  • Gave out client name before NDA/compliance check
  • Call under 4 minutes (didn't complete the flow)
  • Ghosted a candidate (no response for 48+ hours)
📅 Daily / Weekly / Monthly Rhythms
☀️ Daily
  • Source 10+ candidates per active req
  • Complete all scheduled screen calls
  • Update CEIPAL with every interaction
  • Check in with BDMs on active submissions
  • Respond to all candidate messages within 24 hours
📆 Weekly
  • L10 meeting: present your numbers, raise issues
  • Pipeline review with your lead or Mike
  • Listen to 2 recorded calls (yours or team) for self-improvement
  • Sheila's KPI benchmark: 20 candidate screens per week
🗓️ Monthly
  • Review your call scores — where are you consistently losing points?
  • Skill building: one topic deep-dive (STAR coaching, boolean mastery, counter offer handling)
  • Candidate recycling review: are there candidates from closed reqs who fit new ones?
