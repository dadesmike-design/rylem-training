🎙️ Interview Debrief Framework

Every interview your candidate does is market intelligence. Extract it within 2 hours — or it's gone.

⏱️ Call within 2 hours of every interview

💡 Why Debriefs Matter

📈 Every Interview Teaches You Something
💡
The Intelligence Loop

Every interview your candidate does teaches you something — about the client's process, their preferences, their red flags. Good debriefs make your NEXT submission better. Skipping the debrief means you're flying blind forever.

2 hrs
Maximum time to wait before calling. After that, memory fades fast.
10 Qs
The full debrief takes 10 questions — and about 15 minutes.
100%
Log rate in CEIPAL. Every single debrief, every time.

📞 The Debrief Call — 10 Questions

🎯 Ask These in Order — Don't Skip Any

Don't accept vague answers. Follow up until you have something concrete you can log and use.

  • "How do you feel about the interview? How did it go?"
    Open-ended start. Let them vent or celebrate. Their emotional read tells you a lot about how they presented.
  • "Who were the interviewers? What were their titles?"
    Log these names. This is your intel for future submissions to the same client — you'll know who's on the panel.
  • "What questions did they ask? Technical? Situational? Coding assessment?"
    Build your prep playbook for the next candidate. This is gold — most recruiters never ask this.
  • "What topics were covered that you didn't expect?"
    Surprises reveal what the client actually cares about vs. what the job description says.
  • "Did you get to ask questions at the end?"
    What they asked (and didn't ask) tells you about their engagement level and how well you prepped them.
  • "In retrospect, would you answer anything differently?"
    Self-awareness check. Also surfaces specific gaps in their prep that you can address in future coaching.
  • "Do you feel like you showed your best work? What would you change?"
    A candidate who can't self-assess is harder to coach. Use this to calibrate how much guidance they need.
  • "What was the vibe? Did they seem excited about you?"
    Gut check. Experienced candidates can often read a room — this helps you gauge where things stand before the client gives you formal feedback.
  • "Did they mention timeline or next steps?"
    Timeline intel helps you manage candidate expectations and keep the process moving. Follow up with the client accordingly.
  • "On a scale of 1–10, how interested are you in this role after the interview?"
    Critical. If it dropped below a 7, find out why immediately. Don't let a disinterested candidate ghost you mid-process.

📊 What To Do With The Intel

🔄 Close the Loop — Every Time
  • Log EVERY answer in CEIPAL under the candidate's profile — detailed notes, not just "interview went well"
  • Specific technical questions mentioned? → Add them to your prep docs for the next candidate going into the same client
  • Interviewer names captured? → Add to client notes for future submissions — you'll know the panel
  • Candidate felt unprepared for something? → That's a gap in YOUR prep process. Fix it before the next candidate goes in.
  • Share insights with the team in standup — this intel helps everyone, not just you
🧠
Think of Each Interview as Research

You're building a dossier on this client's interview process. After 3–4 candidates go through, you'll have a prep playbook that's worth its weight in gold for every future submission.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

🚫 What Most Recruiters Get Wrong
Waiting Too Long
Calling 24 hours after the interview is too late. Memory fades. Impressions blur. Call within 2 hours while it's fresh.
Accepting "Good"
Only asking "how did it go?" and accepting "good" as an answer. "Good" is not data. Dig in.
Skipping CEIPAL
Not logging the debrief in the ATS. Your memory is not reliable. Your notes don't help your teammates. Log it.
Wasting the Intel
Not using debrief intel to improve future candidate prep. This is the whole point. Close the feedback loop or you're just doing paperwork.
⚠️
The Standard Is Simple

Every candidate who interviews gets a debrief call within 2 hours. Every debrief gets logged in CEIPAL. No exceptions.
