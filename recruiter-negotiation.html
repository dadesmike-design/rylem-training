🗣️ The Pay Conversation
📞 How to Handle Comp on Screen Calls
  • ALWAYS let the candidate state their number first: "What hourly rate are you targeting?"
  • Never reveal the top of the range
  • If their number is reasonable: "That's in the range we're working with."
  • If too high: "The market for this role is typically $X–Y. Would that work for you?"
  • If too low: Don't correct upward — that's margin you keep
🧮
Salary Conversion

Annual ÷ 2,080 = Hourly Equivalent. A candidate making $100K/year = ~$48/hr. Use this when candidates think in salary terms.

📄 W-2 vs. 1099 vs. Corp-to-Corp
W-2 (Our Standard)
Rylem is the employer of record. We handle taxes, benefits eligible.
1099 — Independent Contractor
Higher rate but no benefits, candidate handles own taxes. Compliance risk — use carefully.
Corp-to-Corp
Candidate has their own LLC/Corp. We pay the corp. Different rate structure.
🛡️ Counter Offer Defense
⚔️ When a Candidate Gets a Counter
  • Raise it BEFORE it happens: "What will you do when your current employer counters?"
  • The stat: 80% of people who accept counteroffers leave within 6 months anyway
  • The reason they wanted to leave doesn't disappear with more money
  • Once you've tried to leave, you're marked — they start looking for your replacement
🚨
Plant the Seed Early

If you wait until the counter happens, it's too late. Bring it up during the first screen so they've already thought it through.

🚩 Rate Negotiation Red Flags
  • Candidate won't give a number — fishing for your max
  • Rate expectation is 30%+ above market — misaligned expectations, will waste your time
  • Candidate says "I'll take whatever" — flight risk, no commitment to the role
  • Comparing W-2 rate to 1099 rate — apples to oranges. Educate them on the difference.
