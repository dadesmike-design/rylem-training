Candidate Objections

🛡️ Top Candidate Objections & How to Handle Them

Every objection is an opportunity to build trust. Know the playbook, own the conversation.

Overview Foundation Execution Acceleration Lead Gen Negotiation Objections Compliance Tech Overview Advanced Sourcing
1️⃣ "I'm not looking right now"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I completely understand. Most of the best candidates I work with aren't actively looking. That's actually why I thought of you — the strongest people are usually employed. I'm not asking you to make a change today. I just want to make sure you know about this opportunity so you can make an informed decision. Can I take 2 minutes to tell you about it?"

2️⃣ "Just send me the job description"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I'd be happy to send details. Before I do, I want to make sure it's actually relevant to what you're looking for. Can I ask you a couple quick questions so I don't waste your time with something that's not a fit?"

⚠️
Why This Matters

This keeps you in control. If you just email the JD, you lose the conversation. They read it, form opinions without context, and ghost you.

3️⃣ "What's the rate / salary?"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"Great question — it depends on experience level and what the full package looks like. What are you targeting?" (Always flip it back)

  • If pressed: "The range we're seeing for this type of role is $X–Y depending on experience. Where do you fall in that?"
4️⃣ "I need to think about it"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"Absolutely. What specifically would you want to think through? I might be able to help with some of those questions right now."

  • Set a specific callback: "How about I call you Thursday at 2pm? That gives you time and we don't lose momentum."
  • NEVER accept "I'll call you back" — set the time yourself
5️⃣ "I'm in the middle of other interviews"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"That's great — it means you're in demand. Where are you in those processes? I ask because timing matters — if we can get you in front of my client this week, you'll have more options to compare."

  • This is intel gathering AND urgency creation in one move
6️⃣ "I've had bad experiences with recruiters"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I hear that a lot, honestly. A lot of recruiters treat candidates like numbers. Here's how we're different — I'm going to prep you for every interview, debrief with you after, and give you honest feedback even if it's not what you want to hear. I'd rather be straight with you than waste your time."

7️⃣ "Can I talk to the hiring manager directly?"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I understand wanting that. Let me get you through the initial screen first — once the client confirms interest, you'll absolutely meet the hiring manager. I want to make sure you're set up for success before that conversation."

8️⃣ "The commute is too far" / "I need remote"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I hear you. Let me check with the client on flexibility. Before I do — if they offered hybrid/2 days, would that change things for you?"

  • Get their real threshold before going to the client — don't waste a favor on the wrong ask
9️⃣ "I don't work with staffing agencies"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I respect that. Can I ask why? I want to make sure whatever experience you had before doesn't happen here."

  • Then address their specific concern
  • Usually it's ghosting, lowballing, or being treated like a commodity
🔟 "Your rate is too low"
💬 Your Response
🗣️

"I appreciate you being direct. Help me understand — is it the rate itself or what you'd net after taxes/benefits? Our W-2 includes [benefits summary]. When you factor that in, the total comp is closer to $X."

  • If they truly want more than market: "I want to get you the best deal possible. The market for this skill set in this area is running $X–Y. If you're looking for $Z, I may have other roles that support that rate. Want me to look?"
